Welcome, Dragons

This is a historic moment for the Ismaili Professionals Network & Business Alliance. Today marks the inaugural Den—our most ambitious entrepreneurship initiative yet. You are witnessing the birth of a platform that will become a defining institution within our community.

We have gathered the most accomplished entrepreneurs, investors, and innovators from our network to sit as Dragons. Your presence, your judgment, and your mentorship will set the standard for excellence that future cohorts will aspire to.

What is The Den?

The Den is inspired by Dragon's Den—a platform where exceptional entrepreneurs pitch their vision to accomplished investors, industry leaders, and innovators. But The Den is distinctly ours. It's built on the principles that have always guided our community: excellence, integrity, mutual support, and creating positive change.

Unlike traditional pitch events, The Den is designed to:

  • Spark genuine connections between founders and mentors
  • Provide rigorous but constructive feedback
  • Open doors to networks, mentorship, and strategic support
  • Raise founder visibility and credibility
  • Strengthen the entrepreneurial ecosystem within the Ismaili community
Note on Investment: Dragons are neither expected nor required to invest during or after this event. If, at your discretion, you wish to explore investment opportunities with a finalist privately, any such discussions occur entirely outside The Den programme and in your personal capacity.

The Journey to Today

Over four months, we conducted a rigorous selection process to identify exceptional founders worthy of standing before you today.

Phase 1: Open Call & Applications (November 2025)

Entrepreneurs at all stages—from early idea to scaling venture—submitted comprehensive written applications covering their idea, problem, uniqueness, traction, impact, and vision. Deadline: 28th November 2025.

Phase 2: Independent Judging (December 2025 – January 2026)

A panel of independent judges—respected entrepreneurs, investors, and business leaders—scored all applications anonymously and objectively on a rigorous 0–5 scale across five equally weighted dimensions.

Phase 3: Shortlisting (February – April 2026)

Top-scoring applicants were invited to pitch live to you today. We ensured diversity of industry, stage, and impact to give you a range of exceptional founders.

These finalists have already impressed a rigorous panel of independent judges. You are seeing only the best.

The 5 Evaluation Dimensions

You will score each founder on a 0–5 scale across five equally weighted dimensions. These are the same dimensions used in the independent judging phase.

1Idea & Business (20%)

What you assess:

  • Clarity and memorability of pitch
  • Reality and validation of problem
  • Competitive differentiation

2Progress & Traction (20%)

What you assess:

  • Evidence of traction (revenue, customers, partnerships)
  • Resilience and learning from challenges
  • Momentum and market validation

3Impact & Responsibility (20%)

What you assess:

  • Social and community benefit
  • Environmental and ethical responsibility
  • Sustainability embedded in model

4Support & Vision (20%)

What you assess:

  • Strategic clarity of support needs
  • 12-month growth plan credibility
  • Realistic milestones and metrics

5The Founder (20%)

What you assess:

  • Domain expertise and credibility
  • Genuine passion and authenticity
  • Communication and coachability

Scoring Scale

Score each dimension on a 0–5 scale. Here's what each score means across all dimensions:

Score Definition
5 Outstanding — Exceptional on this dimension. Strong evidence, clear articulation, compelling vision.
4 Strong — Very good on this dimension. Minor gaps but overall impressive.
3 Solid — Good fundamentals on this dimension. Missing depth in one area but credible.
2 Developing — Some promise but lacks clarity, evidence, or depth on this dimension.
1 Early Stage — Significant gaps or major issues on this dimension.

Key Principles

  • A '3' is solid. It means the founder and business are credible and promising on that dimension.
  • Early-stage is not a penalty. An idea-stage founder with exceptional vision can score higher than a revenue-stage founder lacking differentiation.
  • All five dimensions are equally weighted. Don't favour one dimension more heavily.
  • Your average score across all five dimensions = that founder's overall score.
  • These are the same founders independent judges already evaluated. Your scores reflect how they perform live vs. on paper.
