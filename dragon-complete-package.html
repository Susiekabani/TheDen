What is The Den?

The Den is inspired by Dragon's Den—a platform where exceptional entrepreneurs pitch their vision to accomplished investors, industry leaders, and innovators. But The Den is distinctly ours. It's built on the principles that have always guided our community: excellence, integrity, mutual support, and creating positive change.

Unlike traditional pitch events, The Den is designed to spark genuine connections, provide rigorous but constructive feedback, and open doors to networks, mentorship, and strategic support.

Our Philosophy

  • We invest in entrepreneurs—not just ideas. Passion, resilience, authenticity, and vision matter as much as market size.
  • Impact is fundamental. Whether economic, social, or environmental, we seek founders building for purpose—not just profit.
  • Feedback is a gift. Every founder deserves constructive insight—even those who don't win this week.
  • Mentorship compounds value. Your guidance may prove as transformative as capital ever could.
  • We believe in our people. The Den is about strengthening the entrepreneurial ecosystem within the Ismaili community.
Important Note on Investment:

Dragons are neither expected nor required to invest during or after this event. If, at your discretion, you wish to explore investment opportunities with a finalist privately, any such discussions occur entirely outside The Den programme and in your personal capacity.

The Journey to This Week

Over several months, we conducted a multi-stage selection process to identify exceptional founders worthy of standing before you. All finalists were selected in early 2026.

Phase 1: Open Call & Written Applications

We launched The Den with an open invitation to our community. Entrepreneurs at all stages—from early idea to scaling venture—were invited to apply. Applicants submitted comprehensive written applications covering their idea, problem, uniqueness, traction, impact, and vision.

Phase 2: Independent Judging

A panel of independent judges—respected entrepreneurs, investors, and business leaders—reviewed all applications and created a first-pass shortlist based on written submissions. This was a rigorous screening, but not yet the final selection.

Phase 3: Video Pitch Stage

Shortlisted founders submitted 3–5 minute video pitches. This stage was crucial because it revealed who could communicate effectively, stay composed under pressure, and articulate their vision dynamically. Shortlisted candidates were then also judged by four judges on their video pitch.

Importantly, strong video performers were not always the top written scorers. Some founders shine on paper but struggle live. Others—articulate, compelling, authentic—come alive on camera.

Phase 4: Finalist Selection

Based on a holistic review of both written applications and video pitches, a final cohort of founders was selected to pitch live to you this week. These are founders who showed promise both on paper and on camera—founders who can handle the pressure of live Q&A.

Your Role This Week:

You are not just scoring pitches. You are gatekeepers of excellence, providers of wisdom, network architects, community builders, and history makers. These founders have impressed us through written applications and video pitches. Now, they face you.

The Five Evaluation Dimensions

You will score each founder on a 0–5 scale across five equally weighted dimensions (20% each). Each dimension has been informed by the written application framework, adapted for live pitch evaluation.

1

Idea & Business (20%)

What you assess:

  • Clarity and memorability of pitch
  • Reality and validation of problem
  • Competitive differentiation
  • Market insight and opportunity size
2

Progress & Traction (20%)

What you assess:

  • Evidence of traction (revenue, customers, partnerships)
  • Resilience and learning from challenges
  • Momentum and market validation
  • Execution quality and speed
3

Impact & Responsibility (20%)

What you assess:

  • Social or community impact focus
  • Environmental or sustainability practices
  • Responsibility and values alignment
  • Long-term positive outcomes vision
4

Support & Vision (20%)

What you assess:

  • Strategic clarity of support needs
  • 12-month growth plan credibility
  • Realistic milestones and metrics
  • Ambition aligned with execution
5

The Founder (20%)

What you assess:

  • Domain expertise and credibility
  • Genuine passion and authenticity
  • Communication and coachability
  • Resilience and leadership under pressure

Scoring Guide

Score each founder on a 0–5 scale. This scale applies across all five dimensions.

Score Definition
5
 Outstanding — Exceptional on this dimension. Strong evidence, clear articulation, compelling vision.
4
 Strong — Very good on this dimension. Minor gaps but overall impressive.
3
 Solid — Good fundamentals on this dimension. Missing depth in one area but credible.
2
 Developing — Some promise but lacks clarity, evidence, or depth on this dimension.
1
 Early Stage — Significant gaps or major issues on this dimension.
0
 No Answer / Not Relevant — Founder does not address this dimension.

Key Scoring Principles

  • A '3' is solid. It signals a credible founder with real capability.
  • Early-stage is not a penalty. A founder with compelling vision and learning mindset can score higher than a revenue-stage founder lacking differentiation.
  • Don't confuse polish with substance. A polished pitch with vague traction may score lower than a rough-around-the-edges founder with real evidence and momentum.
  • All five dimensions are equally weighted. Don't favour one; give fair consideration to each.
  • Your average score = overall founder score. Add up your five scores and divide by 5.
  • Be consistent and fair. Score based on what you observe, not your pre-formed opinions.

Your Feedback Matters

Write 1–2 sentences. Be specific, constructive, actionable. Your words may stay with a founder for years.

Strong feedback example:

"Your customer validation is compelling, but your go-to-market strategy needs stress-testing. Consider running a pilot with 3 anchor customers to refine your acquisition cost and retention model before scaling."

What makes it work:
  • Specific: names the exact area needing work
  • Constructive: offers a concrete next step
  • Forward-looking: frames growth, not criticism

The Flow of the Evening

  1. Welcome & Dragon Introduction — Connect with your fellow judges
  2. Youth Entrepreneur Pitches — Emerging founders share their vision (not scored)
  3. Finalist Pitch Rounds — Each finalist delivers a 5-minute pitch, followed by 10 minutes of Dragon Q&A
  4. Immediate Scoring — Score privately using your scoresheet
  5. Dragon Deliberation — Discuss, weigh scores, identify winners
  6. Winner Announcement — Celebrate the exceptional founders

While They Pitch (5 minutes)

  • Listen for clarity, authenticity, and conviction. Do they command the room?
  • Assess execution discipline. Can they distil their vision into five sharp minutes?
  • Look beyond polish. Does their passion feel genuine? Is there substance beneath the delivery?
  • Note what stands out: market insight, founder credibility, differentiation, or genuine purpose.

During Q&A (10 minutes)

You have ten minutes. Use them. Ask what matters to you. Probe where you see gaps. Challenge assumptions. Watch how they respond under pressure.

  • Ask the questions only you would ask. Your experience is your edge.
  • Dig into validation. How did they discover this problem? Who validated it?
  • Explore differentiation. Why them? What's their unfair advantage?
  • Challenge on traction. What proof points do they have? How did they acquire them?
  • Understand ambition. Where do they see this in 24 months? Are they realistic yet bold?
  • Watch resilience. When pressed, do they defend thoughtfully or get defensive? Are they coachable?

Immediately After (2–3 minutes)

  • Capture your instinctive assessment. First impressions matter.
  • Score across all five dimensions on your scoresheet.
  • Jot 1–2 sentences of substantive feedback. Be specific. Be constructive. Be memorable.
  • Score what you observe, not what you expect or wish to be true.
