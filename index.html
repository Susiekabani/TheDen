Frequently Asked Questions

About the Documents

Q: Which document should I read first?
A: Start with the Dragon Briefing Pack. It provides essential context about The Den, the selection journey, and your role. Then move to the Scoring Rubric Explained for concrete scoring examples.

Q: Can I print the documents?
A: Absolutely. All Word documents are print-friendly with professional formatting. The Excel scoresheet is designed for both digital and printed use.

Q: Is the HTML website mobile-friendly?
A: Yes. The website is fully responsive and works on phones, tablets, and desktops. Bookmark it for easy reference.

About Scoring

Q: What if I'm not sure about a score?
A: The Dragon Scoresheet has all 5 score definitions built in at the top. Refer to the specific dimension's criteria and compare the founder to the descriptions. The Scoring Rubric Explained document also provides detailed examples.

Q: Are all five dimensions weighted equally?
A: Yes. Each dimension is worth 20%. Your average across all five dimensions equals that founder's overall score.

Q: Is a score of 3 considered good or bad?
A: A 3 is solid and respectable. It means the founder and business are credible and promising on that dimension. A 3 does not indicate weakness.

Q: What if a founder is early-stage? Should I penalise them?
A: No. Early-stage is not a penalty. An idea-stage founder with exceptional vision, credibility, and learning mindset can score higher than a revenue-stage founder lacking differentiation or resilience.

About the Event

Q: How much time do I have to score?
A: You'll have a brief pause after each founder's Q&A (approximately 2–3 minutes) to score immediately whilst fresh. The scoresheet is designed for quick entry.

Q: Can I ask clarifying questions if something is unclear?
A: During the Q&A (5 minutes), absolutely. Ask probing questions to clarify their vision, traction, or strategy. The Event Guide has sample questions to get you started.

Q: What should my feedback say?
A: Keep it to 1–2 sentences. Be constructive, specific, and actionable. For example: "Your market validation is strong, but consider developing a clearer go-to-market strategy. Speaking with 10 target customers about your acquisition approach would help." The Event Guide has more examples.

About Investment

Q: Do I have to invest as a Dragon?
A: No. Dragons are not expected or required to invest during or after the event. Investment discussions, if they happen at all, occur privately and entirely outside The Den programme.

Q: Can I mentor finalists after the event?
A: Yes, if you wish. Any mentorship or investment discussions would occur in your personal capacity, outside The Den.

General

Q: Where can I get technical support if I have issues?
A: Contact the event organiser (details will be provided separately). For questions about scoring or the documents, refer to the Scoring Rubric Explained or Event Guide.

Q: Can I share these documents with other Dragons?
A: Yes, please do. The more prepared all Dragons are, the better the evaluation process. Share digitally or print copies.
